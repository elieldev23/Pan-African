Exploring the History of Pan-African & Transnational Feminism

Welcome

This digital project explores Pan-African and transnational feminism by tracing its historical origins and highlighting some of the key women who shaped its development. It shows how African, Caribbean, and African American women have long understood their struggles as connected across colonial borders.

“Pan-African feminist histories remind us that Black women have always organized across borders, imagining freedom beyond empire.”

How did Black women in the United States, the Caribbean, and Africa build transnational feminist networks that connected struggles against racism, colonialism, and gender oppression from the early 20th century onward?

Use the navigation above to read about:

  • Origins – how Pan-African feminism emerged from anti-colonial struggles and women’s organising.
  • Key Figures – short portraits of thinkers and activists whose work advanced Pan-African feminist ideas.
